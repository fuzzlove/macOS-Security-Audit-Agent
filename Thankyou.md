# Acknowledgements

Mac Audit Agent would not be what it is today without the contributions, research, standards, feedback, and open-source work of countless individuals, organizations, and communities dedicated to improving cybersecurity for everyone.

We would like to extend our sincere appreciation to:

### The Open Source Security Community

Thank you to the developers, researchers, maintainers, and contributors who dedicate their time to building tools, publishing research, sharing knowledge, and advancing the state of defensive security. Your willingness to openly collaborate continues to make the security community stronger.

### Objective-See Foundation

Special thanks to Patrick Wardle and the Objective-See community for their outstanding work in macOS security research and the development of high-quality open-source security tools. Projects such as ProcessMonitor, FileMonitor, BlockBlock, KnockKnock, TaskExplorer, OverSight, and many others have helped educate and inspire generations of defenders and researchers.

### Apple Security

Thank you to Apple and the engineers responsible for macOS security, platform hardening, Lockdown Mode, System Integrity Protection, Transparency Consent and Control (TCC), Gatekeeper, XProtect, Endpoint Security Framework, and the many technologies that continue to raise the security baseline for users worldwide.

### MITRE Corporation

Thank you to the MITRE Corporation for maintaining the CVE Program, ATT&CK Framework, and related security resources that have become foundational standards for security analysis, vulnerability tracking, and incident response.

### National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)

Thank you to NIST for developing and maintaining the National Vulnerability Database (NVD), Cybersecurity Framework (CSF), Risk Management Framework (RMF), and numerous publications that continue to guide both public and private sector cybersecurity programs.

### Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)

Thank you to CISA for providing actionable guidance, vulnerability advisories, the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) Catalog, and public resources that help organizations better understand and respond to emerging threats.

### Security Researchers Everywhere

Thank you to independent researchers, bug hunters, penetration testers, incident responders, forensic analysts, educators, students, and hobbyists who continually work to identify vulnerabilities, improve defenses, and share their findings responsibly.

### Educators and Mentors

Thank you to the instructors, authors, conference speakers, trainers, and community leaders who dedicate themselves to teaching others and helping the next generation of cybersecurity professionals grow.

### Testers and Early Users

Thank you to everyone who installs, tests, critiques, reports bugs, suggests features, and provides feedback. Every issue reported, every recommendation made, and every improvement suggested helps make Mac Audit Agent a better tool for the community.

### Families, Schools, and Organizations

Thank you to the parents, teachers, administrators, caregivers, researchers, and organizations who continue to advocate for safer technology and stronger security practices for everyone.

## Our Mission

Mac Audit Agent is built around a simple idea:

**Security should be understandable, accessible, transparent, and available to everyone.**

Whether you are a parent protecting a family computer, a student learning cybersecurity, a security analyst investigating an incident, a researcher exploring macOS internals, or an organization working to improve its defensive posture, we hope this project helps make security more approachable and effective.

Thank you to everyone who contributes, supports, tests, educates, and shares knowledge with others.

Together, we make the ecosystem safer.
